Hello, I'm Mayan, 24 years old. My dream since I was 16 was to fly to Japan with my best friend from high school. This dream took a while to realize due to army service and a toxic relationship that lasted five years.

After the breakup, I finally flew to Japan with my best friend for a month and ten days, which were simply amazing. It was an experience I'll never forget. On top of everything, it was my first solo flight (without my parents). I met my friend in Osaka, after I took a domestic flight myself from Tokyo to get there. It gave me confidence for the future, showing me I'm capable of flying alone anywhere I want, but of course, it's more fun to fly with friends. I'm glad I took this step as a new beginning for my life.

The website essentially shows some photos from the trip, details about each photo and the experiences we had, and tips on how to manage in Japan.

Tokyo Highlights üèôÔ∏è

Tokyo is the capital of Japan.
It has a bit of everything: parks, culture, clubs, food, anime, and more.
It's a city where you could easily spend a long time without ever getting bored.
I was there for three weeks, and it was near the end of my travel itinerary.
I recommend keeping Tokyo for the end of your trip to Japan, as you'll likely fly home from its main international airport.

Kyoto is a wonderful blend of culture and nature.

Osaka Food & Fun üêô

Osaka is the city of food culture and my absolute favorite city.
It's like Tokyo, but smaller, and the atmosphere there is more local and less touristy.
Most people love Tokyo, but I fell in love with Osaka the people, the food, and everything it has to offer.
On my next trip, I will definitely extend my time there.

Nara's Deer Park ü¶å

Nara is famously known as the city of deer.
It's not an overly touristy destination; most travelers primarily visit the central deer park, where the animals roam freely. You can purchase special crackers to feed the deer, but be careful they can sometimes be a bit aggressive!
These deer are super cute and will even bow to you, and the park itself is absolutely stunning.
